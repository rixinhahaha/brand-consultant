# What Your Charm Says About You — Slow Build Editorial
**Format:** Instagram Reel / TikTok Video | **Duration:** 18s | **Scenes:** 5
**Ad Copy:** Every charm has a meaning. This one is yours. The anchor. The moon. The star. Each Alex and Ani charm carries researched symbolism — not decoration, but intention. Choose the one that speaks to who you are right now.
**CTA:** Find Your Charm

## Brand Visual Direction
This variation treats the video as a fashion editorial brought to life — every frame could freeze and live on a magazine page. The camera is mostly static or on extremely slow, imperceptible movements. The woman is not just wearing jewelry; she is the subject of a portrait series. Light is soft but intentional, modeled beautifully on skin and metal. The palette is restrained: champagne, warm cream, the specific gold of the brand (#ab8c52), and the warmth of natural skin without any competing hues. Pacing is unhurried and deliberate — each scene breathes fully before cutting.

## Generation Prompt
> Vertical 9:16 editorial format, shot primarily on a locked-off or near-static camera with very slow, imperceptible movements — this video breathes at the pace of a long exhale. Opens on a static overhead extreme close-up: a gold anchor charm bangle on a champagne fabric surface, light falling from a single large soft source to the left — no harsh shadows, only a gradual transition from lit to shadow that reveals the texture of both the metal and the fabric beneath it. A woman's hand with natural warm skin tone enters from the right edge of frame and lifts the bangle with the deliberateness of someone who has done this a hundred times and means it each time. Cut to a wide medium shot, camera static and slightly elevated — the woman sits or stands in a neutral warm interior, the bangle sliding onto her wrist in the lower portion of the frame while her composed face occupies the upper half; she does not look at camera yet, her gaze is downward and inward, a moment that belongs to her. The montage that follows treats each charm with its own dedicated still frame: moon charm on a wrist in a locked-off close-up, camera motionless while the bangle settles into stillness; cut to star charm, same composed approach; cut to heart charm held toward soft light, the composition balanced and still — each shot held for a full second before cutting, each text overlay appearing with the precision of typeset text on a magazine spread. The direct address scene is shot from a slight elevation, the woman occupying the bottom two-thirds of the frame with negative space of warm cream wall above her, a rare and composed wide version of a direct address — she does not lean into camera, camera does not move toward her; she speaks from a grounded, composed position. The final product scene is the most editorial of all: a precisely arranged but not over-styled grouping of charm bangles on champagne fabric, shot static overhead, the Alex and Ani wordmark placed in the clean negative space above the bangles. Color grade: warm champagne, slightly lifted shadows, golden highlights restrained to glow rather than bloom, clean and editorial throughout. Music: sparse acoustic guitar transitioning to a single held ambient note beneath the final scene.

## Scene 1 — The Still (3s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Extreme close-up
- **Camera angle:** True overhead — perfectly vertical, 90 degrees to the surface
- **Camera movement:** Locked-off entirely — no movement; the hand entering the frame is the only motion
- **Framing:** Bangle placed at perfect center-frame; hand enters from the right, cutting across the lower half of the frame; strong geometric composition — the circle of the bangle against the rectangle of the fabric

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Large soft source to camera-left — approximately the size of a large window with full diffusion; gradual, directional but not harsh
- **Quality:** Soft, with clear directionality; warm white at approximately 5000K (cooler than golden hour but still warm); shadow side of bangle falls away from camera-right
- **Mood:** Clean, considered — the light of an editorial studio that still reads as natural; respectful of the subject

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** Gold anchor charm bangle on champagne or warm cream fabric surface; woman's hand with natural warm skin tone
- **Action:** Bangle rests perfectly still for the first 1.5 seconds; hand enters and lifts it; the action is unhurried — no rushing to fill the 3 seconds
- **Expression:** No face — the composition itself carries the editorial weight

### Environment
- **Background:** Champagne or warm cream fabric — slightly textured, not wrinkled but not pressed either; no other props or elements
- **Depth of field:** Moderate, f/2.8 equivalent — bangle fully sharp, fabric texture rendered, no background to blur

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Warm gold (#ab8c52) bangle on champagne — a near-monochromatic warm palette
- **Grade:** Warm champagne base; shadows lifted to champagne rather than black; highlights restrained — gold glows rather than blooms; slightly desaturated midtones for editorial restraint

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** None
- **Music:** Single sparse acoustic guitar note or ambient tone — minimal, editorial
- **Text overlay:** "This isn't just a bracelet." — clean serif, warm gold (#ab8c52), appears on cut, lower third, precisely typeset

## Scene 2 — The Portrait (4s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Medium shot — wider than typical; includes head to mid-thigh
- **Camera angle:** Slightly elevated — camera positioned approximately 30cm above eye level, looking down gently
- **Camera movement:** Static — locked-off entirely; subject moves, camera does not
- **Framing:** Woman positioned in lower-center; negative space of warm cream or champagne wall above and to the sides; wrist action visible in lower third of overall frame; her face in upper-center

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Large soft source from camera-left — same quality as Scene 1; models beautifully on her face with a gradual shadow transition; not flat, but gentle
- **Quality:** Soft, directional; warm 4800K; subtle bounce fill from camera-right at very low ratio — primarily one-light aesthetic
- **Mood:** Portrait session — she is being observed; the wider framing gives her room to exist in the frame as a person, not just a wrist

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** Woman, late 20s, simple warm-toned clothing (no busy patterns), minimal layered gold jewelry including necklaces
- **Action:** Slides anchor bangle onto wrist; her gaze is downward and interior — she is not playing to camera; a private moment extended
- **Expression:** Focused inward — the slight smile is barely there; the editorial equivalent of a quiet moment caught

### Environment
- **Background:** Warm cream or champagne wall — clean, smooth, editorial; possibly a linen or plaster texture that adds warmth without becoming a prop
- **Depth of field:** Moderate, f/3.5 equivalent — full figure moderately sharp; background slightly separated

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Warm skin tone, gold bangles, cream clothing, champagne background
- **Grade:** Same editorial champagne grade; skin tones warm and clean; no color cast in the shadow side — simply darker version of the warm midtone

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** Warm female voice, measured and even: "The anchor. It means you're someone who stays grounded — no matter what."
- **Music:** Guitar continues, sparse; no new elements yet
- **Text overlay:** None (VO carries the scene; editorial composition does not compete with text)

## Scene 3 — Charm Stills (4s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Close-up (each cut) — tighter than Scene 1 and 2
- **Camera angle:** (a) Slightly elevated looking down at wrist — 30-degree angle from horizontal; (b) Eye level to the wrist — true horizontal; (c) Slightly below wrist looking up, heart charm against soft light background
- **Camera movement:** All three shots locked-off — absolutely static; the only motion is the bangle settling or the hand raising the charm; stillness is the editorial statement
- **Framing:** Each shot is precisely composed — wrist and charm in the lower two-thirds, deliberate negative space above; no cropping anxiety, each frame has room

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Same large soft source for (a) and (b); (c) uses a large soft light from behind and above, making the heart charm glow slightly at its edges
- **Quality:** Soft, editorial throughout; no specular highlights that read as artificial; metal catches light in a controlled, glowing way
- **Mood:** Considered, still — the editorial equivalent of a typeset word: placed and intentional

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** (a) Lighter warm skin tone, moon charm bangle; (b) Deeper warm skin tone, star charm stacking; (c) Medium warm skin tone, hand holding heart charm
- **Action:** (a) Moon bangle is already settled on wrist — barely any motion; (b) Star bangle completes its slide onto the wrist in the first 0.5 seconds, then holds still; (c) Hand is already raised with heart charm in position — barely any motion; the action is near-complete at cut
- **Expression:** No faces — the stillness of the hands after motion has the editorial dignity of a finished gesture

### Environment
- **Background:** (a) and (b): warm cream or champagne, out of focus; (c): soft warm light from behind creating a near-white warm background for the charm
- **Depth of field:** Shallow, f/2.0 equivalent — charms sharp, backgrounds clean and soft

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Gold charms on varied warm skin tones; all backgrounds warm and neutral
- **Grade:** Consistent editorial champagne grade; slight warmth difference in (c) from the soft backlight — permitted

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** None
- **Music:** Guitar pluck synced precisely to each cut — three notes, each clean and unhurried
- **Text overlay:** Appears on each cut: (a) "Moon. Intuition." (b) "Star. Courage." (c) "Heart. Love you give freely." — clean serif, warm gold (#ab8c52); placed with editorial precision — not lower-third default but positioned relative to negative space in each composition

## Scene 4 — Composed Address (4s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Wide medium — widest camera-to-subject framing in the piece
- **Camera angle:** Slightly elevated — same as Scene 2; camera above her eye line looking down gently
- **Camera movement:** Static — locked-off; this compositional choice is the editorial statement: the camera does not pursue her; she speaks to it from a composed position
- **Framing:** She occupies the bottom third of the frame; warm cream wall occupies the upper two-thirds as negative space; wrist with bangles just visible at the lower frame edge; the negative space above her is intentional and editorial

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Same large soft source from camera-left; her face well-lit but not front-lit — dimension visible in the soft transition to shadow; warm, dignified
- **Quality:** Soft, directional; warm 4800K; consistent with the rest of the piece
- **Mood:** Grounded, spacious — the negative space above her creates the sense of a woman with interior room; she fills the frame from the bottom, which is a position of groundedness not vulnerability

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** Same woman; wrist visible at bottom of frame with stacked bangles
- **Action:** Speaks to camera from her composed, still position; does not move toward camera; does not gesture extensively — she is still, her words are the action
- **Expression:** Resolved — the editorial version of certainty; calm and absolute

### Environment
- **Background:** Warm cream or champagne wall, clean and smooth; the negative space is an active design element
- **Depth of field:** Deep, f/4.0 equivalent — both her face and the clean background wall are sharp; the editorial aesthetic requires depth, not shallow-focus separation

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Warm skin tone, gold bangles, cream clothing, champagne background — the most restrained palette in the piece
- **Grade:** Same editorial grade; the wide framing means more background in frame — the champagne wall grade must be consistent

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** Sync to lip movement: "I didn't pick this one because it's pretty. I picked it because it's mine."
- **Music:** A soft ambient tone or sustained acoustic note beneath her speech
- **Text overlay:** None — the editorial composition and the wide frame make text unnecessary and cluttering

## Scene 5 — Editorial Product (3s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Overhead medium close-up
- **Camera angle:** True overhead — 90 degrees to the surface; precisely vertical
- **Camera movement:** Locked-off — entirely static; the most static shot in the piece
- **Framing:** Bangles arranged in lower-center of frame with deliberate intentionality — not flat-lay styled but editorially composed; the Alex and Ani wordmark occupies the clean negative space in the upper half of the frame

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Same large soft source from camera-left; from overhead, the light rakes across the bangles at a slight angle — enough to reveal the texture and charm detail without hard shadows
- **Quality:** Soft, editorial — the cleanest, most controlled lighting moment in the piece
- **Mood:** Final statement — the product stands on its own in the light

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** Three to four gold charm bangles on champagne fabric; Alex and Ani wordmark
- **Action:** No movement — entirely static; the stillness after 17 seconds of (restrained) motion is itself an editorial choice
- **Expression:** Product — metal quality and charm detail presented with full editorial dignity

### Environment
- **Background:** Champagne fabric surface — consistent with Scene 1; continuity of material environment
- **Depth of field:** Deep, f/4.0 equivalent — all bangles in sharp focus; editorial requires full sharpness throughout

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Gold (#ab8c52) bangles on champagne — near-monochromatic, consistent with Scene 1 bookend
- **Grade:** Consistent editorial champagne grade; wordmark in warm off-white or champagne against the fabric

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** None
- **Music:** Resolves to a single held ambient note or silence; the music does not swell or become more than it was
- **Text overlay:** "Every charm has a meaning. Find yours." — serif, warm gold, placed in negative space of upper frame alongside/beneath wordmark; CTA: "Find Your Charm" — sans-serif, off-white

## Negative Prompts
- No cool-toned or silver jewelry — all metal must be warm gold
- No white or grey backgrounds — champagne, cream, and warm skin tones only
- No flat-lay hands with perfect manicures or stock photography hand poses
- No spiritual cliches: no crystals, incense, tarot cards, or candles
- No artificially generated or obviously synthetic-looking hands or wrists
- No handheld camera shake or drift — all shots must be static or imperceptibly slow
- No fast cuts, quick montage, or kinetic editing rhythms
- No busy backgrounds or environmental props that compete with the jewelry
- No forced smiles or performed confidence — expressions must be interior and natural
- No crowded compositions — negative space is a non-negotiable design element throughout
- No music that builds to an emotional swell or becomes more than background texture

## Post-Production Notes
- Color grade: warm champagne editorial — shadows lifted to a warm champagne rather than crushed; highlights restrained to a warm glow not a bloom; midtones slightly desaturated for editorial cleanliness; all cool tones removed
- Text overlays appear on cut with no animation — typeset precision is the aesthetic
- Serif typeface in warm gold (#ab8c52) for charm names and headline copy; sans-serif off-white for CTA
- Guitar plucks in Scene 3 must be frame-accurate to each cut
- Captions burned in for sound-off viewing — placed to respect the negative space compositions; do not default to centered lower-third if it disrupts the editorial framing
- All transitions are hard cuts — no dissolves except the wordmark in Scene 5 which may dissolve in over 1 second
- The Scene 4 wide negative-space composition must be held as-shot — do not crop or reframe in post
- No additional stabilization needed in post — locked-off shots require none
- Sound design: fabric rustle and metallic chime in Scene 1 should be subtle and present but never compete with the music or VO; environmental audio throughout should feel restrained
