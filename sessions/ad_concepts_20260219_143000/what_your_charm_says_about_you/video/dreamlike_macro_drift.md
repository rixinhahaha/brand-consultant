# What Your Charm Says About You — Dreamlike Macro Drift
**Format:** Instagram Reel / TikTok Video | **Duration:** 18s | **Scenes:** 5
**Ad Copy:** Every charm has a meaning. This one is yours. The anchor. The moon. The star. Each Alex and Ani charm carries researched symbolism — not decoration, but intention. Choose the one that speaks to who you are right now.
**CTA:** Find Your Charm

## Brand Visual Direction
This variation exists at the intersection of memory and material — a world where the camera gets close enough to a charm that its surface becomes a landscape, and close enough to skin that the light plays across it like a slow-moving weather system. The mood is soft focus at the edges, pin-sharp at the point of intention. Everything moves at a fraction of its natural speed — not slow motion in the action-sports sense, but the natural drift and settle of objects and bodies when they are treated as worth watching. The palette is amber dream: warm gold, honeyed skin, cream that glows rather than sits. This is jewelry as inner life made visible.

## Generation Prompt
> Vertical 9:16 format, shot on a fluid-head macro-capable system with a tilt-shift or macro lens for selective plane-of-focus effects; all camera movement is slow, continuous drift — no cuts within scenes, and between scenes hard cuts land on the most compressed, abstracted moment of each composition. Opens on an extreme macro close-up of the gold anchor charm's surface — the anchor symbol fills the entire frame, texture of the gold visible as if it were terrain; a slow drift left reveals the edge of the charm bangle, and at the very edge of the drift, a warm hand enters and the bangle is lifted in a single continuous motion, the camera following the lift with a slow upward tilt. The cut reveals the woman from the mid-section down — the camera is positioned at wrist height, looking slightly up; the bangle slides onto her wrist in half-speed slow motion, the stacked bangles shifting and settling; the camera drifts from her wrist up toward her face over the duration of the scene, arriving at a soft close-up as she glances down. The montage is three near-abstract wrist close-ups — each shot an extreme macro that begins on the charm's surface detail and slowly widens to reveal it is on a wrist; the charm's texture and the skin's texture given equal weight; each cut is a compression from maximum abstraction to barely revealed subject. The direct address scene uses a racking focus technique: the wrist and bangles are in sharp focus in the foreground while the woman's face is soft behind them; the rack focus slowly, deliberately, moves the sharp plane from the bangles to her face over the first two seconds, and she speaks as the focus arrives on her — as if the camera is deciding to pay attention to what she says. The final product scene is the most abstract macro in the piece: extreme close-up of the charm surface and the fabric beneath it, the camera drifting slowly across as if reading the surface, before pulling back to an overhead that reveals the full bangle grouping and the wordmark. Color grade: amber-dream — warm, soft, slightly luminous; shadows are warm and lifted to a honeyed amber; highlights bloom gently at the warm end; a subtle diffusion effect at the frame edges throughout.

## Scene 1 — Surface and Lift (3s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Extreme macro — the anchor charm's surface fills the frame; transitions to close-up as bangle is lifted
- **Camera angle:** Begins horizontal (macro lens parallel to charm surface); tilts upward slowly as bangle is lifted
- **Camera movement:** Slow leftward drift beginning from the charm's center, revealing the bangle edge; then a slow upward tilt tracking the lift of the bangle; continuous fluid movement throughout — no static moment
- **Framing:** Opens on: the anchor symbol fills the entire frame — we may not immediately know what we are looking at; by the end of the 3 seconds, the framing has revealed it is a bangle being lifted by a hand

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Large, extremely diffused soft source from camera-left — almost shadowless; the goal is to reveal the texture of the charm's surface through the quality of light on its relief, not through shadow
- **Quality:** Very soft, wrap-around, approximately 4800K; a slight warm push in the background; the charm surface catches the light in its engraved details
- **Mood:** Reverent and abstracted — the light makes the charm's surface look like something worth examining at this scale

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** Gold anchor charm bangle — the charm itself is the landscape; then the woman's hand (warm skin tone) enters and lifts the bangle
- **Action:** Camera drifts left across the charm surface, revealing the bangle edge; hand enters; bangle is lifted slowly — approximately half the natural speed of lifting; camera tilts up with the lift
- **Expression:** No face — the charm surface and the warmth of the hand are the subject and the emotion

### Environment
- **Background:** At macro distance, the cream surface beneath the bangle falls into soft warm blur; the background is entirely out of focus
- **Depth of field:** Near-macro depth of field — the in-focus plane is approximately 2–5mm deep; only a sliver of the charm surface is sharp at any given moment as the camera drifts

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Warm gold (#ab8c52) charm surface in extreme detail, warm skin entering frame, cream blur
- **Grade:** Amber-dream grade — warm base, shadows lifted to honeyed amber, highlight bloom at the warm end; slight diffusion filter effect at frame edges (not center); slightly soft contrast curve

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** None
- **Music:** Sparse, resonant — a single guitar pluck or ambient warm tone; the sound design includes a very subtle metallic chime that feels like it comes from the charm itself
- **Text overlay:** "This isn't just a bracelet." — clean serif, warm gold (#ab8c52), appears on cut at the moment the hand enters frame; lower third

## Scene 2 — The Slow Stack (4s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Begins at close-up of wrist, ends at medium close-up of face
- **Camera angle:** Begins at wrist height looking up slightly at approximately 20 degrees; slowly tilts upward over 4 seconds to arrive at face height, eye level
- **Camera movement:** Fluid-head continuous upward tilt — a slow reveal from wrist to face; not a pan, a tilt; the camera is on a fixed position and rotates slowly upward; at no point is the camera static
- **Framing:** First 2 seconds: wrist and bangles in center frame, upper frame is her torso in soft blur; last 2 seconds: face arrives in center frame, wrist now in lower frame in soft blur; the tilt creates a natural narrative of looking first at the object, then at the person

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Natural window light from camera-left; soft and directional; as the camera tilts up, the light quality changes slightly — her face catches more of it than the wrist did
- **Quality:** Soft, warm, 4800K; the quality of light that makes skin and gold look like they belong in the same frame
- **Mood:** Discovery — the tilt from object to person is a visual metaphor for the concept; the bangle leads to the person, the person gives the bangle meaning

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** Same woman — wrist and bangles in the first half, face in the second half
- **Action:** She slides the anchor bangle onto her wrist in the first half of the tilt; her expression is private; as the tilt arrives at her face she glances down at her wrist with a small, inward smile
- **Expression:** At face arrival: the inward, private smile of someone in a moment that belongs to them — not performed for camera; the camera's slow arrival makes this feel witnessed, not directed

### Environment
- **Background:** Warm interior, soft blur throughout; the background changes as the tilt moves — from whatever is behind her wrist to whatever is behind her face; both should be warm, neutral, and fully out of focus
- **Depth of field:** Shallow throughout, f/1.8 equivalent — the plane of focus is whichever element (wrist or face) the camera is currently looking at; during the tilt transition, both are soft

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Gold bangles catching warm light, warm skin tone, champagne ambient
- **Grade:** Consistent amber-dream grade; the shift from wrist to face in the tilt should be seamless in grade

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** Warm female voice, slightly hushed — a quality of intimacy that matches the dreamlike visual: "The anchor. It means you're someone who stays grounded — no matter what."
- **Music:** Soft guitar, continuing; slightly more atmospheric than Scene 1 — possibly a second instrument or a soft pad
- **Text overlay:** None

## Scene 3 — Charm Abstractions (4s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Extreme macro on each cut — beginning fully abstract, slowly widening to reveal
- **Camera angle:** (a) Camera parallel to moon charm surface — looking directly at the face of the charm; (b) Camera looking up from below the star bangle's underside; (c) Camera close enough to the heart charm that the reflection of the light source is visible in the metal surface
- **Camera movement:** (a) Slow outward drift (rack focus pulling back to reveal the charm is on a wrist); (b) Slow clockwise rotation drift — barely perceptible — while widening; (c) Lateral drift left, slowly widening; all movements fluid, no static moments
- **Framing:** Each shot begins as near-abstraction — the charm surface is what we see; by the end of each 1.3-second shot, just enough of the wrist and bangle has been revealed to identify what we were looking at

### Lighting
- **Key light:** (a) Overhead diffused soft light — even on the moon surface; (b) Slight backlight from below — the underside of the bangle catches warm ambient; (c) Direct soft source to the left — the reflection of the light source visible as a warm orb in the heart charm's surface
- **Quality:** Soft throughout, warm; the macro distance means the slightest variation in light direction has a dramatic effect on what the charm surface looks like
- **Mood:** Contemplative, symbolic — at macro distance, the charms begin to look like objects from a mythology; the visual language supports the idea that these symbols carry meaning

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** (a) Moon charm on lighter warm skin tone wrist; (b) Star charm stacked on deeper warm skin tone wrist; (c) Heart charm on medium warm skin tone, held toward light
- **Action:** Each shot: the camera slowly pulls back from abstraction to reveal, while the wrist beneath the charm remains mostly still — the motion is the camera's, not the subject's; in (c) the fingers holding the charm may sway very slightly
- **Expression:** No faces; the charm surface and the quality of the skin beneath it

### Environment
- **Background:** (a) Warm cream blur at macro distance; (b) Warm amber blur — slightly darker, more dramatic; (c) Blown-out warm light from the source in the background — the heart charm is against near-white warm ambient
- **Depth of field:** At macro distance: extremely shallow — 2–5mm depth; the in-focus plane moves as the camera pulls back, transitioning from the charm surface to a point on the bangle as the reveal proceeds

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Gold charms in extreme close focus, warm skin tones, warm blurred backgrounds
- **Grade:** Same amber-dream grade; (c) backlit heart charm should have a warm lens-glow effect at its edges — the light wrapping around the charm

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** None
- **Music:** Guitar pluck synced to each cut — three notes; here the plucks feel like they reverberate longer than usual, trailing into the macro dream quality
- **Text overlay:** (a) "Moon. Intuition." (b) "Star. Courage." (c) "Heart. Love you give freely." — clean serif, warm gold; appears on cut, centered rather than lower-third, to work with the abstract compositions

## Scene 4 — Rack Focus Address (4s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Medium close-up with selective focus shift
- **Camera angle:** Eye level — camera exactly at her eye height
- **Camera movement:** Locked-off — the camera does not move; the focus rack is the movement; begins on the bangles in foreground (she is soft behind them), slowly racks to her face over 2 seconds, holds sharp on her face for the remaining 2 seconds as she speaks
- **Framing:** Her face in the upper half of the frame; her wrist and bangles in the lower foreground, slightly closer to the camera; both elements share the frame simultaneously; the rack focus shifts the visual emphasis from the object to the person

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Natural window light from camera-left; both her face and the bangles receive the same quality of light; at f/1.4 equivalent, only one can be sharp at a time
- **Quality:** Soft, warm, 4800K; the light is consistent whether the focus plane is on the bangles or her face — the light does not change, only what we see changes
- **Mood:** The slow rack focus creates a meditation on the concept itself — we look at the charm, and then we look at the person, and the connection between them is the whole point of the ad

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** Woman (same as all prior scenes); wrist with stacked bangles in the immediate foreground
- **Action:** For the first 2 seconds (rack-to-face): she is still, waiting — only slightly soft in the background; at the moment the focus arrives on her face she speaks, as if the camera's attention gave her permission; her wrist remains in the lower foreground in soft focus throughout
- **Expression:** The specific expression of being about to say something true — a breath; then as focus arrives, the settled, certain look of someone who means it

### Environment
- **Background:** Warm cream or champagne; soft, warm, consistent with the rest of the piece
- **Depth of field:** Extreme shallow, f/1.4 equivalent — this is what makes the rack focus dramatic; the transition from bangles-sharp to face-sharp requires the shallowest possible depth of field

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Gold bangles in near-foreground (briefly sharp at the open), warm skin, warm ambient
- **Grade:** Same amber-dream grade; the soft-to-sharp transition on her face should reveal a warm, luminous skin tone — the grade makes the sharpness feel like a gift

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** Sync to lip movement — begins exactly as focus arrives on her face: "I didn't pick this one because it's pretty. I picked it because it's mine."
- **Music:** Has reduced to a single held ambient tone — atmospheric and warm; her voice is primary
- **Text overlay:** None — the rack focus is the visual event; text would compete destructively

## Scene 5 — Macro to Overview (3s)

### Shot & Camera
- **Shot type:** Opens on extreme macro; ends on overhead close-up — a pull-back reveal
- **Camera angle:** Begins parallel to the charm surface (horizontal macro); slowly tilts and pulls back to arrive at a near-overhead angle
- **Camera movement:** Continuous fluid pull-back and tilt — a slow, smooth move from macro abstraction to product reveal; arrives at the overhead position just as the wordmark resolves in frame
- **Framing:** Opens: a charm surface fills the frame — abstracted gold; ends: three to four bangles on cream fabric, overhead, the Alex and Ani wordmark in the upper portion of the frame; the pull-back covers this entire range

### Lighting
- **Key light:** Same soft source from camera-left; as the camera pulls back and tilts, the light rakes more horizontally across the bangles — at the final overhead position, it catches each bangle's curve and charm detail in a line of warm highlight
- **Quality:** Soft, warm; the quality transitions slightly as the angle changes — more directional in the final overhead position
- **Mood:** The pull-back from abstraction to the full product is the visual equivalent of stepping out of a reverie — the camera returns to the world from the dream

### Subject & Action
- **Subject:** One of the charm bangles (beginning); three to four charm bangles on cream fabric (end)
- **Action:** The camera performs the entire action — continuous pull-back and tilt; the bangles and the surface are still
- **Expression:** Product — in the final overhead position, the charm details are visible and the quality of the gold is evident in the light

### Environment
- **Background:** At macro open: warm cream blur; at the final overhead: the cream fabric surface, slightly textured, real — consistent with Scene 1's opening environment
- **Depth of field:** Transitions from extremely shallow (macro) to moderate (f/2.8 equivalent at the final overhead); during the pull-back, the depth of field expands as the camera moves further from the subject

### Color & Grade
- **Palette:** Gold bangles, cream fabric, warm ambient — the bookend to Scene 1's palette
- **Grade:** Same amber-dream grade; the diffusion effect at frame edges may be slightly reduced in the final overhead to allow the wordmark to read cleanly

### Text & Audio
- **Voiceover:** None
- **Music:** Resolves to a final warm chord or held ambient note — the dream gently concluding
- **Text overlay:** Alex and Ani wordmark appears in the upper frame as the pull-back arrives at the overhead position — resolves in as the camera settles; "Every charm has a meaning. Find yours." — serif, warm gold; CTA: "Find Your Charm" — sans-serif, off-white

## Style References

- Terrence Malick nature-detail inserts — extreme close-ups of objects as visual poetry

## Negative Prompts
- No cool-toned or silver jewelry — all metal must be warm gold
- No white or grey backgrounds — cream, warm neutral, and amber blurs only
- No flat-lay hands with perfect manicures or stock photography poses
- No spiritual cliches: no crystals, incense, tarot cards, or candles
- No artificially generated or obviously synthetic-looking hands or wrists
- No handheld camera shake — all motion must be fluid, continuous, and smooth
- No fast cuts or rhythmic montage editing that breaks the dreamlike pace
- No cool color grading — the grade must remain in the warm amber-dream range throughout
- No anamorphic lens flares or digital lens artifacts — any flare must be organic and warm
- No sharp, clinical-quality macro photography — the macro should always have a soft, warm quality to it
- No rack focus that is too fast — the Scene 4 rack must be slow enough to be meditative, not abrupt
- No hard, artificial shadows — lighting should wrap and be near-shadowless throughout

## Post-Production Notes
- Color grade: amber-dream — warm, soft, slightly luminous; shadows lifted to a warm honeyed amber (not black, not pure champagne — amber); highlights allowed to bloom softly at the warm end; a subtle diffusion effect applied at frame edges (not a vignette — a diffusion; think: pro-mist or TIFFEN filter look, applied digitally); midtones warm and clean
- The rack focus in Scene 4 must be executed or simulated to feel continuous and analog — not a digital depth-of-field transition; the transition should take the full first 2 seconds
- Text overlays: Scene 3 text appears on cut, centered, serif warm gold; all other text appears on cut, lower-third, serif warm gold for charm names and headline, sans-serif off-white for CTA
- Guitar plucks in Scene 3 must be frame-accurate to each cut
- Captions burned in for sound-off viewing — placed sensitively relative to the macro compositions; do not default to centered lower-third if it obscures the charm surface abstraction
- The Scene 5 pull-back camera move should be smooth enough that the transition from macro abstraction to product overhead feels like waking slowly, not a fast reveal
- All camera movements across the piece: confirm in post that no movement was too fast or jarring — if any drift exceeds the intended slow quality, slow it further in post
- Sound design: subtle metallic chime in Scene 1 should feel like it comes from inside the macro world of the charm — spatially intimate, not external; use reverb to create this quality
- Music: if a soft pad instrument is used beneath the guitar, it should be below -20db in the mix — background texture only, never competing with the guitar or VO
